The Photographer

I look for the
unguarded moment.

Photography, for me, is an act of listening. I wait for the world to lower its guard — for that fraction of a second when a person forgets the camera exists and simply is.

Each image I make is an attempt to preserve not just what something looked like, but what it felt like to be there. A mood. A silence. An unfinished sentence.
